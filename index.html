Before you explore

Non-custodial design

Users interact with smart contracts directly; the protocol is not designed to take custody of wallet assets.

Zero-knowledge layer

zk-SNARK proofs support private withdrawals, but privacy is not absolute and user behavior can still create traceable links.

Compliance context

The U.S. Treasury announced a Tornado Cash delisting on March 21, 2025, while warning users to exercise caution around risky transactions.

Tornado Cash FAQ

What is Tornado Cash?

Tornado Cash is a non-custodial privacy protocol for EVM assets that uses smart contracts and zero-knowledge proofs to reduce public linkage between deposits and withdrawals.

Is Tornado Cash a swap?

No. Tornado Cash is not a DEX or token swap; it is a privacy tool, so users should verify the app flow and legal obligations before interacting.

What is the official Tornado Cash app?

The official Classic app entry point referenced by the docs is app.tornado.cash; verify the domain before connecting a wallet.

Is Tornado Cash legal to use?

Legal treatment varies by jurisdiction and transaction context; the U.S. Treasury delisted economic sanctions on March 21, 2025, but users remain responsible for compliance.

Which networks does Tornado Cash support?

The docs describe deployments across Ethereum and several EVM networks; confirm current network and token support inside the official app before using it.
