Tornado Cash app and privacy pools

Tornado Cash is a non-custodial privacy protocol for crypto transactions. Its docs explain that users deposit supported assets into smart contracts and later withdraw to a different address using zero-knowledge proofs, breaking the public on-chain link between deposit and withdrawal. Classic Tornado Cash uses fixed-amount pools, while Tornado Cash Nova introduced arbitrary-amount and shielded-transfer functionality on Gnosis Chain. The core user intent is not generic safety; it is how the app creates privacy, what assets and chains it supports, and how the note or private key controls withdrawal access.

Tornado Cash supported networks and tokens

Tornado Cash documentation lists classic pools on Ethereum for ETH, DAI, cDAI, USDC, USDT, and WBTC. It also describes deployments on BNB Smart Chain for BNB, Polygon for MATIC, Gnosis Chain for xDAI, Avalanche for AVAX, Optimism for ETH, and Arbitrum One for ETH. The docs state that all classic pools operate as fixed-amount deposit and withdrawal pools, with each token having a small set of supported denominations. Users should verify the current app interface and contract addresses because many mirrors and forks exist.

Tornado Cash OFAC sanctions status

The U.S. Treasury removed economic sanctions against Tornado Cash on March 21, 2025, after legal and policy review following litigation. Treasury's delisting statement still warned that it remains concerned about North Korea-linked hacking and money laundering and that U.S. persons should exercise caution around transactions that present such risks. This is a high-value search query because older articles still say Tornado Cash is sanctioned. The current accurate summary is that Tornado Cash was sanctioned in August 2022 and delisted by Treasury on March 21, 2025, while related criminal cases and compliance concerns remain separate issues.

Tornado Cash TORN token and governance

TORN is the Tornado Cash governance token. The docs say TORN lets users participate in protocol governance and that governance and mining smart contracts were deployed by the community. Historical anonymity mining ended before the current period, and Tornado Cash governance has also faced major security incidents, so TORN should not be treated as a simple fee-share token. Searchers researching TORN usually need token market data, governance context, and legal background. The token's price and liquidity are separate from whether the immutable privacy contracts continue to exist on-chain.

Tornado Cash relayers and withdrawals

Tornado Cash withdrawals require a proof that the user controls a valid deposit note without revealing which deposit is being spent. A relayer can help withdraw to a fresh address without that address already holding gas, which improves privacy because the destination does not need a prior funding transaction. The docs recommend relayers as one privacy-preserving step, but users still need careful operational hygiene: avoid linking wallets, timing, amounts, IP addresses, or exchange accounts. Losing the deposit note or private key can mean losing access to the deposited funds.

Tornado Cash FAQ

What is Tornado Cash?

Tornado Cash is a non-custodial privacy protocol that uses smart contracts and zero-knowledge proofs to break the public link between a deposit address and a withdrawal address. It was originally built for Ethereum and later deployed on several EVM-compatible networks.

Is Tornado Cash still sanctioned?

The U.S. Treasury removed economic sanctions against Tornado Cash on March 21, 2025. That does not remove all legal or compliance risk. Treasury still warned about transactions that may benefit malicious cyber actors or North Korea, and criminal proceedings involving developers are separate from the delisting.

What tokens does Tornado Cash support?

Tornado Cash docs list classic Ethereum pools for ETH, DAI, cDAI, USDC, USDT, and WBTC. They also describe deployments for BNB on BNB Smart Chain, MATIC on Polygon, xDAI on Gnosis Chain, AVAX on Avalanche, and ETH on Optimism and Arbitrum One.

What is a Tornado Cash note?

A Tornado Cash note is the secret information generated when a user deposits into a pool. It is needed to withdraw the funds later. If the note is lost, access can be lost. If the note is exposed, someone else may be able to withdraw the deposit.

What is Tornado Cash Nova?

Tornado Cash Nova is a later Tornado Cash pool design released in beta in December 2021. The docs describe it as using arbitrary amounts and shielded transfers on Gnosis Chain, unlike classic fixed-denomination pools that require deposits and withdrawals in preset sizes.

Is Tornado Cash custodial?

No. Tornado Cash is designed as a non-custodial smart contract protocol. Users keep control through their deposit note or private key rather than handing funds to a mixer operator. Non-custodial design does not remove smart contract, legal, operational privacy, or note-management risks.
